Running `plugins/sdd-quality-loop/scripts/emit-run-record.sh epic-159-pillar-a` for this retrospective (`reports/runs/RUN-20260714T193722Z-epic-159-pillar-a.json`) reproduces the same defect and surfaces a second, independent one in the same script:

- **Frequency:** Confirmed across 2 retrospective periods (epic-136-phase1-guards predicted it; epic-159-pillar-a reproduces it with line-level root-cause evidence for both the `total` and the newly-discovered `blocked` defect), and within this period the mis-association affects all 4 of this feature's tasks identically (one script bug, systematically wrong for every task).
- **Phase:** measurement / retrospective tooling (the run-record emitter that WFI verification horizons and cross-feature attribution depend on).
- **Confidence:** High (recurs across ≥2 periods; corroborated by direct script-source line citations, not inference).
- **Do Not Overfit:** This is a narrowly-scoped counting-association bug in one script, not a sign that quality-gate reports themselves are unreliable — every gate report's own `VERDICT:` line, evidence matrix, and Critical Review Cycles content were read and cross-checked directly for this retrospective's Metrics and Review Gate Metrics tables, which do not depend on `emit-run-record.sh`'s counters.
